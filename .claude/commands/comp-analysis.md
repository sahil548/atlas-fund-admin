# Comparable Transaction Analysis

Find and analyze comparable transactions for: $ARGUMENTS (company name, sector, deal type)

## Steps

1. **Search** for recent M&A, investment, and financing transactions in the same:
   - Sector/industry
   - Geography (if relevant)
   - Deal size range (0.5x to 3x the target)
   - Time period: last 3 years

2. **Build comp table**:
| Date | Target | Acquirer/Investor | Deal Size | EV/EBITDA | EV/Revenue | Other Multiple | Premium | Notes |

3. **Analyze the data**:
   - Median and mean multiples
   - Range (25th to 75th percentile)
   - Outliers and why they're different
   - Trend over time (are multiples compressing or expanding?)

4. **Benchmark the deal**:
   - Where does our deal fall vs comps?
   - If above median: justify the premium
   - If below median: identify what discount factors exist
   - Implied valuation range based on comp multiples

5. **Public market comps** (if applicable):
| Ticker | Company | Market Cap | EV/EBITDA | EV/Revenue | Growth | Margin |
   - Trading multiples vs transaction multiples gap
   - Liquidity discount considerations

## Output
Professional comp analysis with sourced data. Use web search to find real transactions. Flag where data is estimated vs confirmed. End with: "Based on comps, the fair value range for this deal is $X–$Y, implying the proposed entry at $Z represents a [premium/discount] of N%."
